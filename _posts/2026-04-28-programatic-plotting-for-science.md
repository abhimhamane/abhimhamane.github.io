This is more of a note to myself for now and future reference.

## TLDR; Plot intentionally... avoid defaults... 

This also came as an advise from my supervisor during one of our internal discussion. It helps (i) convey ideas coherently to someone who is not constantly thinking about your stuff... even if they are your PI and an expert, it helps when things being presented are clear, not clutered, visible(no one should need to squint to see... bare minimum), represent things honestly, follows convention (either formal or informal) and in general looks little asthetic.

I will show only those figures/plots that I made by myself. So you will find all shades of gray, progressing from bad -> acceptable -> intentional (maybe you have a differing opinion). So in a way also document my progress. Initially, I thought that this is not part of science or it's not my job or it's just a mundane beurocratic thing... Maybe something like in school how some kids would decorate their books, while mine were not.

Then what changed my mind?

There were couple of things actually.
1. Attending presentation delivered by others on topics I had zero to very little knowledge. 

2. Reading research papers with default plots, and most egregious pixalated screnshots or bad aspect ratio.

3. Attending university lectures with screnshots in the name slides or all together copied slides (no name calling) but they were some of India's prestigious institute.

So I have been the victim to these academic offences, might have englicted it upon my supervisors or other attendes a few times till now. But later on I do not want to be a repeat offender. I hope you too relate to being victims of above mentioned incidents.
