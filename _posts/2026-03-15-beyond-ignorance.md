I remember, was preparing for a master's degree program interview at the IIT Bombay's Centre for Technology Alternatives for Rural Areas (CTARA) not because I wanted to work in rural areas but because they offered courses on Geospatial analysis and opportunity to learn more on geospatial data processing be it in a more applied sense. Before the interview stage we were required to do a task and write an essay/motivation letter. As I assume many students, I started mine with the fomous quote from Gandhi bapu - "The soul of India lives in its villages.". So as any interview goes, I was asked about my essay and the professor said, something on lines of - okay but what do you think of Dr. B.R.Ambedkar believing - villages to be the "den of ignorance"  and "cesspool of casteism". And I was blissfully unaware of this statement. Long story short, I was not selected for the masters program, although i got something far better at IIT Kanpur.

This thing stuck in my mind and I do have to agree with Ambedkar. For all practical purposes one can call me a "city-boy". My parents did made a point to keep us connected to our village roots, they still do. My understanding of Marathi and spending my kindengarten years in our village aanganwadi and tagging along my cousins to their upper classes did help. Later on as I grew and our biannual visits (Diwali and summer holidays) to my extended family in villages of Solapur district. I saw a big difference in my life in cities/towns of Ahmedabad, Porbandar, Sambhajinagar (earlier Aurangabad), also Nagpur or Kanpur v/s my cousins.
